Identity

Design case study

Identity is a flexible visitor management and appointment platform that enables brands to secure their facility, reduce cost and improve visitor experience.

Identity asked me to help redesign identity to make it all clearer, minimal and most importantly, easier for their users to use with little or no tech support from them.

A significant amount of firms use identity in Nigeria, including facebook’s own hub here in Lagos, so it was really paramount that the design of this product be in the latest trends of UI design for it to maintain the successful commercial form its in.

By making the site comprehensive, easy to navigate, and focusing on prospects, the redesign aims to increase the impact of identity.tech on the bottom line.

New minimal outlook

Less is more

For the redesign of the new UI of identity, I played around with a white space minimalist feel in other to offer less distractions.

This makes it for the user to focus on the page’s purpose and emphasise on the calls to action bringing about an higher rate of engagement especially with users with very short attention span.

And from a development view, it’s easier to build a responsive layout (adaptive to almost any device).

Process

Research & Personas

For some designers, ux is an extra homework and chore. They just want to get into the design of the Hi-fidelity prototypes and dazzle their clients.

While I wont deny of being guilty of this before, experience on designing complex softwares has taught me that the recipe for getting stuck and frustrated while designing is the absence of a solid user experience design before beginning prototypes

For this project, I started out first of all by reaching out to the current users of this product and created a survey on how they feel about the whole flow and design of the product’s current interface. From the data gotten, I deduced that most felt the UI could be better and the dashboard could be less clustered to make navigation more easier. I discussed my findings with the team and later went on to build user personas and user flows to find a solution to the problem.

User flow map

After the team were convinced on the solutions I proposed to the existing ux problems, I moved into making a user flow map in other to get an effective way of working out how I want the users to use and navigate around the interface

Wireframe

With the user flow agreed on, I went on to create the skeletal framework of the product’s UI for the purpose of arranging the different elements to best accomplish the goal of this redesign process: simplicity.

Prototype design

After I have finalized and perfected the layout and flow of the interface with the team, then did I start the design of the Hifi prototypes. While I don’t pride myself as an overlord in illustration :), I am quite comfortable creating simple and minimal characters which I used for this project just to help maintain consistency throughout the design, giving it a clean and crisp UI.
